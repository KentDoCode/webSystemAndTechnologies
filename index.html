My Autobiography

Introduction

I am Kent Villalun, a simple yet passionate student from Puro, Magsingal, Ilocos Sur. This is a glimpse into my life, from where did all started, what are my learnings so far, and what are my goals or things I want to achieved.

Early Life

I was born on November 3, 2005 at Bantay, Ilocos Sur. I was raised by a family that is full of love and always supportive in things that I want. I never dreams of becoming a programmer, or anything related to IT. As a child, I always wanted to be like a policeman, an engineer, or a doctor. But as growing up, experienced technology, my interest changed, and now, I wanted to be a developer, to make my own games, websites, and applications

Education

Currently, I am 3rd year student at University of Northern Philippines, and taking BS in Information Technology. subjects may be hard at times but I am enjoying it because I know that it is the path that I want.

Hobbies and Interests

I love playing video games, especially those immersive graphics and good storyline. I also enjoyed learning on my own, like web development. I am currently familiarizing myself with CSS and soon to start Javascript. Sometimes, I also play the guitar for fun, because I am not good at it.

Life Challenges

There are times of burnout, especially when acads, problems, and other responsibilities all come at the same time. But what's important is that I am learning, I learned how to take a break, and trusting that everything is happening for a reason.

Dreams and Goals

My end goal is to become a software engineer, and maybe work abroad someday or even start my own tech company. I want to help and provide for my family and be able to pay back their sacrifices.

Life is a journey, and I'm still in the beginning of mine. But one thing I know is for sure, I'll keep on learning, keep on moving forward, and keep on chasing my goals in life. If there is anything I want you to remember from me. That is no matter how hard life is. You must always keep on thriving, keep on moving forward because it's worth it.
